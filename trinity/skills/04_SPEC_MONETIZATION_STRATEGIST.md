### Example 2: Pricing Experiment (A/B Test Results)

**Query:** "Should we price Professional at €249 or €299?"

**Skill Response:**
```
EUFM PRICING EXPERIMENT: PROFESSIONAL TIER
Test Period: Weeks 4-8 (Month 2)
Sample Size: 400 Freemium users (200 per variant)

VARIANT A: €249/month
- Conversions: 12 / 200 = 6.0% conversion rate
- MRR: 12 × €249 = €2,988
- Perceived value: "Good deal" (feedback survey)

VARIANT B: €299/month
- Conversions: 10 / 200 = 5.0% conversion rate
- MRR: 10 × €299 = €2,990
- Perceived value: "Fair price" (feedback survey)

STATISTICAL ANALYSIS:
- Revenue difference: €2 MRR (negligible)
- Conversion rate difference: 1 percentage point
- P-value: 0.34 (NOT statistically significant)

RECOMMENDATION: Choose €299/month

REASONING:
1. **Revenue neutral** in short term (€2 difference)
2. **Higher perceived value** (€299 suggests "professional" positioning)
3. **Upgrade path clarity** (€249 → €799 is 3.2x jump; €299 → €799 is 2.7x, feels more natural)
4. **Future pricing flexibility** (easier to discount from €299 → €249 than raise)
5. **Market positioning** (competitors charge €400-800; €299 positions as "best value")

SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS:
- At 100 customers: €299 generates €29,900 MRR vs €24,900 at €249 = €5K/month difference (€60K/year)
- At €299, we can offer 16% discount (€249) for annual prepay and still match monthly pricing economics

ORACLE VALIDATION (Perplexity search):
- "SaaS pricing psychology" research: Prices ending in "99" perceived as "premium but fair"
- "B2B SaaS churn rates": €249-299 range has similar churn (12-14% annually)
- "EU consultant spending habits": Target persona budgets €300-500/month for tools

DECISION: Set Professional tier at €299/month.

Action: Update pricing page, email current trial users with new pricing (grandfathered at old rate for 6 months to maintain trust).

Constitutional note: Pricing transparency maintained. All users informed of reasoning.
```

### Scripts to Include

**`scripts/calculate_revenue_projections.py`**
- Input: Customer count per tier, pricing, conversion rates, churn rate
- Algorithm: Monte Carlo simulation (10,000 iterations) with P30/P50/P70 quantiles
- Output: Monthly MRR projections, ARR, cash flow, confidence intervals

**`scripts/run_pricing_experiment.py`**
- Input: Experiment ID, variant A/B prices, sample size, duration
- Algorithm: Track conversions, calculate statistical significance, assess perceived value
- Output: Winning variant + recommendation + reasoning

**`scripts/optimize_conversion_funnel.py`**
- Input: Funnel stage data (visitors → signups → trials → customers)
- Algorithm: Identify bottlenecks (largest drop-offs), suggest improvements
- Output: Prioritized optimization opportunities with expected impact

**`scripts/calculate_customer_ltv.py`**
- Input: Subscription tier, average retention months, upsell probability
- Algorithm: LTV = (Monthly price × Retention months) + (Upsell value × Upsell probability)
- Output: LTV per tier, LTV:CAC ratio, customer segment profitability

**`scripts/generate_marketing_plan.py`**
- Input: Target customer acquisition, available budget, channels (SEO, ads, webinars)
- Algorithm: Allocate budget across channels based on historical CAC per channel
- Output: Month-by-month marketing plan with expected leads/conversions
