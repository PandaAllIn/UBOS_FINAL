## SUCCESS FACTORS (BASED ON EU HISTORICAL DATA)

### What Wins Grants

**Top success factors (in order of importance):**

1. **Quantitative rigor (30% of success variance)**
   - Every claim backed by data (Data Commons, Wolfram, credible sources)
   - Clear methodology (show your work, not just results)
   - Oracle provenance (demonstrates thoroughness)

2. **Evaluator language (25% of variance)**
   - Use exact terminology from call text (if call says "TRL 7-9", don't say "mature technology")
   - Structure proposal to match evaluation criteria (Innovation 30%, GHG 30%, etc. â†’ structure sections accordingly)
   - Answer the question asked (evaluators score what's in the criteria, not what's impressive)

3. **Consortium strength (20% of variance)**
   - Partners with proven track record (â‚¬6M UBOS success = credibility)
   - Geographic balance (multi-country consortia score higher for Horizon Europe)
   - Complementary expertise (don't duplicate, demonstrate synergy)

4. **Risk mitigation (15% of variance)**
   - Acknowledge risks honestly (evaluators spot BS, appreciate transparency)
   - Concrete mitigation actions (not generic "we'll manage it carefully")
   - Contingency plans (what if drilling hits unexpected geology? what if GPU prices spike?)

5. **Replication potential (10% of variance)**
   - Template thinking (this project creates model for X other regions)
   - Market size quantification (addressable market: 180M people, 42 GW capacity)
   - Policy alignment (helps EU achieve X directive, Y strategy, Z target)

### What Kills Grants

**Top failure factors (avoid these):**

1. **Generic proposals (40% of failures)**
   - Copy-paste from previous applications
   - Ignore call-specific priorities
   - Use buzzwords without substance ("innovative", "cutting-edge", "world-class" without evidence)

2. **Weak quantification (25% of failures)**
   - Vague claims ("significant emissions reduction" vs "237,000 tonnes COâ‚‚e annually")
   - No source attribution ("studies show..." without citation)
   - Math errors (evaluators check calculations)

3. **Unrealistic plans (20% of failures)**
   - Overly ambitious timelines (12-month datacenter build = instant rejection)
   - Underestimated budgets (â‚¬10M for 100 MW facility = not credible)
   - Ignore implementation barriers (assume all permits approved instantly)

4. **Poor writing (10% of failures)**
   - Jargon without explanation
   - Inconsistent terminology
   - Formatting errors (page limits, font sizes, submission file types)

5. **Wrong program (5% of failures)**
   - Apply for Innovation Fund with TRL 3-4 technology (not ready)
   - Apply for Horizon Europe with purely commercial project (no research component)
   - Ignore eligibility criteria (non-EU partners in widening country calls)
