## 5.2 Risk Analysis and Mitigation Strategies

### Technical Risks

**Risk T1: Geothermal Reservoir Underperformance**
- **Description:** Wells produce lower flow rates or temperatures than expected
- **Probability:** Low (BƒÉile Felix extensively characterized, TRANSGEX SA operational history)
- **Impact:** Medium (reduced cooling capacity, higher PUE)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Drill contingency well (4th production well budgeted)
  - Over-design absorption chiller capacity (60 MW cooling for 50 MW data center load = 20% margin)
  - Contract terms: TRANSGEX SA performance guarantees (minimum flow/temperature contractual obligations)
- **Residual Risk:** Low

**Risk T2: Absorption Chiller Performance Shortfall**
- **Description:** Chillers achieve COP lower than specification (0.5 instead of 0.65), increasing geothermal demand
- **Probability:** Low (mature technology, manufacturer performance guarantees)
- **Impact:** Low-Medium (require additional geothermal wells, slightly higher PUE)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Factory acceptance testing before shipment (validate performance at design conditions)
  - Manufacturer warranty (performance guarantees, replacement if targets not met)
  - Backup cooling: Traditional chillers on standby (operate only during maintenance or peak loads)
- **Residual Risk:** Very Low

**Risk T3: System Integration Challenges**
- **Description:** Geothermal loop + absorption chillers + data center cooling integration more complex than anticipated
- **Probability:** Medium (novel integration at this scale)
- **Impact:** Medium (delays commissioning, cost overruns for modifications)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Detailed engineering phase (18 months pre-construction validation)
  - Digital twin simulation (test integration virtually before physical construction)
  - Phased commissioning (test each subsystem independently before full integration)
  - Experienced integration contractor (hire firm with geothermal + data center experience)
- **Residual Risk:** Low-Medium

**Risk T4: Data Center IT Equipment Reliability in Geothermal-Cooled Environment**
- **Description:** Higher humidity or temperature variations affect server reliability
- **Probability:** Low (chilled water systems provide precise temperature control)
- **Impact:** Low (isolated to specific racks, manageable with redundancy)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Industry-standard temperature/humidity controls (15-25¬∞C, 40-60% RH maintained)
  - Redundant cooling zones (N+1 cooling capacity)
  - Standard server hardware (hyperscaler-proven equipment, not experimental cooling methods)
- **Residual Risk:** Very Low

**Risk F1: Funding Gap (Innovation Fund Awards Less Than Requested)**
